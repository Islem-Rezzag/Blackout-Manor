# Character Turnarounds V2

## Character Direction

The cast should read as ten distinct stylized humans at a masked gothic manor party. They are not tokens, beans, astronauts, toys, or sci-fi avatars. Each suspect needs a recognizable silhouette from whole-manor scale and a premium portrait read during meetings.

Character art must communicate public state only. It may show observed emotion, posture, injury/death state, speaking state, vote state, and meeting expression. It must never reveal private chain-of-thought, hidden roles before public reveal, or private model intent.

## Shared Silhouette Rules

- Distinguish suspects by shoulder width, coat/skirt length, hat/hair mass, cape/shawl, mask outline, accessory size, and walking posture.
- Do not rely only on color.
- Keep human proportions. Slight stylization is fine; capsule bodies are not.
- Use a readable head/torso/leg structure at whole-manor scale.
- Preserve a clean top silhouette for room crowding.
- Keep accessories large enough to read but not so large that they obscure nearby doors, bodies, or tasks.
- Portrait and world sprite must share the same mask, hair, accessory, accent color, and posture bias.

## Mask And Accessory Rules

- Every suspect gets one mask profile: half mask, long-nose mask, narrow domino, lace mask, metal-rim mask, feathered edge, mourning veil, theatre mask, scholar spectacles/mask hybrid, or cracked porcelain mask.
- Every suspect gets one signature accessory: cane, fan, pocket watch, gloves, shawl, ledger, rose pin, opera scarf, key ring, or lantern.
- Accessories should support readable social acting: pointing, hiding, fidgeting, clutching, presenting evidence.
- Avoid weapons as default identity accessories unless future game rules explicitly support them.

## Portrait Rules

- Bust or three-quarter portrait, consistent crop.
- Soft directional warm key with cold storm rim.
- Same costume and mask identity as in-world sprite.
- Eyes visible enough to show suspicion, fear, confidence, and shock.
- Background uses subtle room or curtain cue, not a generic gradient.
- Meeting portraits may be more expressive than roaming sprites but must not become caricatures.

## HEART Surface Emotion Mapping

Use only public or surface emotion states derived for presentation. Do not show private reasoning. Suggested visual mapping:

- calm: neutral stance, soft hands, steady gaze
- alert: forward lean, lifted chin, tighter hands
- suspicious: narrowed eyes, angled shoulders, accessory held as barrier
- shaken: lowered shoulders, hand near chest or mask, uneven stance
- confident: open chest, controlled hand gesture, level gaze
- defiant: planted feet, raised chin, sharp gesture
- fearful: recoil, tighter silhouette, head turned toward threat
- guilty-looking: fidget, averted gaze, guarded posture; this must never confirm actual role

## Posture States

All suspects should support:

- idle calm
- walking north/south/east/west or equivalent directional set
- task interaction
- speaking
- listening
- suspicious glance
- startled/recoil
- meeting seated
- voting/ballot gesture
- accused
- eliminated/dead body silhouette

## Meeting Expression States

Portraits should support:

- neutral listening
- speaking
- accusing
- defensive
- afraid
- angry/defiant
- relieved
- shocked by contradiction
- vote locked
- eliminated/revealed

Expressions are public performance states, not hidden truth states.

## Deception, Suspicion, And Fear Cues

Use social cues without confirming truth:

- Deception-coded performance: fidgeting gloves, smile too still, accessory hiding mouth, a half-step back.
- Suspicion-coded performance: narrowed gaze, turned shoulders, pointing cane/fan, chin drop.
- Fear-coded performance: hunched shoulders, clutching accessory, widened eyes, retreating step.
- Confidence-coded performance: open palms, direct stance, steady eye line.

Never visually mark a suspect as "the murderer" before public reveal.

## Ten Suspect Archetypes

### Iris - The Blue Veil Witness

- Silhouette: tall, narrow, flowing evening gown with long veil trailing behind one shoulder.
- Mask/accessory: silver-blue lace half mask, small opera binoculars.
- Accent: deep sapphire with moon-silver trim.
- Posture bias: upright but observant, head slightly tilted.
- Portrait focus: reflective eyes, veil edge catching cold window rim.
- Meeting expression: quiet certainty, then visible hurt when doubted.
- Motion note: fabric trail follows turns with delayed settle.

### Victor - The Mourning Barrister

- Silhouette: broad-shouldered formal black coat, high collar, squared capelet.
- Mask/accessory: black domino mask, folded legal papers or sealed envelope.
- Accent: bone white and tarnished gold.
- Posture bias: rigid, measured, hands controlled.
- Portrait focus: stern brow, hard side light, papers near frame.
- Meeting expression: precise accusation or cold defense.
- Motion note: restrained steps, strong stop poses.

### Seraphina - The Crimson Hostess

- Silhouette: dramatic red gown, fan shape, high hair or feather profile.
- Mask/accessory: ornate crimson mask, folding fan.
- Accent: claret, brass, black lace.
- Posture bias: theatrical, centered, socially dominant.
- Portrait focus: fan half-covering expression, warm candle edge.
- Meeting expression: cutting smile, sudden alarm when contradicted.
- Motion note: fan snaps open on speaking or accusation.

### Dante - The Green-Coated Gambler

- Accent: dark green, brass, cream shirt.

- Portrait focus: watch chain crossing vest, sideways glance.

- Motion note: quick pivots and coin/watch fidgets.

### Elise - The Gold Salon Heiress

### Jasper - The Violet Stage Magician

- Silhouette: long violet coat, scarf, narrow hat or swept hair shape.
- Mask/accessory: violet theatre mask, cane.

- Portrait focus: cane and half smile, stage-shadow background.

### Lyra - The Black Lace Widow

- Silhouette: dark gown, lace shawl, narrow waist, veil or high collar.

### Felix - The Amber Engineer

- Mask/accessory: amber mask with metal rim, key ring or brass gauge.

- Silhouette: layered teal dress or coat, soft shoulders, botanical hair ornament.

- Accent: teal, greenhouse green, warm brass.
- Posture bias: careful, empathetic, slightly hunched when afraid.
- Portrait focus: rain-glass background, leaf ornament, worried eyes.

### Silas - The Ash Butler

- Silhouette: lean servant-formal tailcoat, narrow tie, straight posture, gloves.
- Mask/accessory: ash-gray mask, key ring or serving tray.
- Accent: gray, silver, dark burgundy.

## Sprite-Sheet And Rig Recommendations

### Preferred Final Direction

Use cutout rigs or authored sprite sheets exported from a consistent turnaround system:

- front, back, left, right, three-quarter variants if budget allows
- separate head, torso, upper arms, forearms, hands, hips, legs, feet, mask, accessory, garment overlays
- authored idle and walk cycles with garment follow-through
- meeting seated and portrait expression layers sharing the same identity kit

### Minimum Runtime Set

For each suspect:

- `idle`
- `walk`
- `task`
- `speak`
- `react`
- `meeting_seated`
- `vote`
- `accused`
- `body`

### Recommended Sheet Structure

- World sprite source: 1024x1024 or 2048x2048 per suspect turnaround sheet.
- Runtime atlas: 512x512 or 1024x1024 per suspect depending on final scale.
- Portrait source: 1536x2048 or larger.
- Runtime portrait: 512x768, with 256x384 fallback.
- Separate expression layers when using cutout/rig pipeline.

## Animation Rules

- Movement should have anticipation, foot contact, and settle.
- Garments and accessories should lag slightly, then settle.
- Meeting reactions should be readable but restrained.
- Fear/suspicion should change posture and eye line before adding UI badges.
- Do not over-animate idle states; subtle breathing and accessory shifts are enough.
- Keep performance browser-first. Avoid huge per-character atlases if a cutout rig or shared animation set can preserve quality.

## Avoid List

- bean, capsule, crewmate, astronaut, helmet-only, or mascot silhouettes
- sci-fi armor, glowing visors, jetpacks, or spaceship uniforms
- generic fantasy villagers
- chibi proportions that erase human social acting
- color-only identity
- identical mask shapes across the cast
- portraits that do not match world sprites
- hidden-role tell colors before reveal
- exaggerated villain lighting on one suspect before public evidence supports suspicion
- gore-forward body states
